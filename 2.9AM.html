Alien & Sedition Acts (1798) -

Passed under John Adams (Federalist)

Alien Act: raised residency for citizenship from 5 → 14 years; aimed to weaken immigrant support for Democratic-Republicans.

Sedition Act: made it illegal to criticize the gov’t → fines & jail for journalists (e.g. James Callender).

Seen as unconstitutional, but upheld by Federalist courts, showed deepening Federalist vs. Democratic-Republican divisions.

Showed deepening Federalist vs. Democratic-Republican divisions -

Goals: strengthen national economy, gain elite support, unify states under federal government.

Debt: fund national debt at face value ($54M) + assume state debts ($21.5M) → tied wealthy creditors to federal government.

Excise Tax (1791): taxed goods (esp. whiskey), hit rural/frontier communities.

Bank of the U.S. (1791): national bank, gov’t major stockholder, could regulate economy & credit.

Jeffersonians: unconstitutional (strict construction), Federalists: constitutional (loose construction, Precedent for federal power expansion.

Whiskey Rebellion (1794) -

Rural PA farmers resisted whiskey tax (whiskey = drink, export, barter currency), tax collectors tarred/feathered.

Washington raised 13,000 militia → rebellion collapsed before fighting, showed strong federal authority under Constitution (contrast w/ Shay’s Rebellion under Articles).
